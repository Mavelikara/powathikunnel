About Us

The Powathikunnel Family — Mavelikara Branch

The Powathikunnel family traces its origins through two distinct lineages — the Varghese and Ulahannan trees — both rooted in the rich heritage of the Mavelikara region of Kerala, India.

AD 52
St. Thomas the Apostle arrives in Kerala, establishing the foundation of Christianity among the Nasrani community from which this family descends.
c. 1750
The Powathikunnel lineage is established with Varghese Vazhappallil as the earliest documented ancestor of the Varghese tree.
AD 1806
Mallappally Varghese Powathikunnel, son of the third generation Tommy, moves with his four sons to settle at Mavelikara, establishing the house name 'Powathikunnel' at its present location. He purchased the Kambisseril and Pulinilkunnathil properties and became a wealthy and influential figure in Mavelikara.
Present
The family has grown to 775+ members across two trees, with branches spread across Kerala, India, and internationally.
